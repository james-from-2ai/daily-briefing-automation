Agentic AI and labor displacement in LMICs

A new paper, "Agentic AI and Occupational Displacement: A Multi-Regional Task Exposure Analysis" (ArXiv 2604.00186), finds that as AI shifts from task-augmentation to task-completion, occupational exposure increases sharply in clerical and professional roles. In developing economies, disruption may materialize faster than productivity gains due to digital infrastructure gaps. A gendered dimension emerges: in East Asia and the Pacific, women are more exposed than men to the AI displacement effect. Only ~10–12% of jobs in East Asia + Pacific involve AI-complemented tasks (vs. 30% in advanced economies), but that minority is more vulnerable in the near term. Paper

So what for 2AI: The gender-displacement finding is underreported and aligns with Anthropic's own labor-market impact research. 2AI has no public position on AI labor impacts in LMICs — this is the "should be publishing but nobody is" gap from 2AI's own thesis.
