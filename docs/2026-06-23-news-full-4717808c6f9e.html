AI in education (LMIC) — Rwanda model + WhatsApp tutors

Rwanda launched Africa's first AI Education Cloud in late 2025, enabling adaptive learning in both Kinyarwanda and English with dashboards for teachers. Rising Academies is piloting WhatsApp-based AI tools — Rori (math tutor), Tari (teaching assistant), and LearnLens (auto-grading) — across Rwandan public schools at very low cost. The World Bank has completed pilots across Côte d'Ivoire, Gambia, Mali, Ghana, Ethiopia, Tanzania, and Mauritius using adaptive learning and WhatsApp-based tutors. An EdTech Monday event in May 2026 focused on Rwanda's emerging AI education approach. The persistent challenge: infrastructure gaps and staff capacity limit how fast these models scale beyond urban centres.

So what for 2AI: Rising Academies' WhatsApp-based tutor model (Rori/Tari) is the closest peer deployment to what 2AI's ed workstream would build. Direct partnership or evaluation inquiry with Rising Academies could accelerate 2AI's evidence base in the education vertical.

National AI policy in LMICs — Kenya, Nigeria, "Next Billion" Index

Kenya is finalising a National AI and Emerging Technologies Policy by June 2026 (per January 2026 announcement), complementing its AI Strategy 2025–2030. The strategy emphasises inclusive AI, local innovation, and data governance. Nigeria's AI Strategy 2025–2029 links AI infrastructure to local talent and social inclusion. An important new dataset: the Next-Billion AI Index (arXiv, June 2026) provides a "compass for AI utility and adoption in the global majority" — the first systematic benchmarking of AI readiness and adoption across non-OECD countries. Over 10 African nations now have active AI strategies (Algeria, Benin, Egypt, Ethiopia, Ghana, Kenya, Mauritius, Nigeria, Rwanda, Senegal, Tunisia, Uganda).

So what for 2AI: The Next-Billion AI Index is a significant new reference dataset for 2AI's cross-country positioning work. Kenya's June 2026 policy finalisation is a potential moment for 2AI to contribute a public comment or position paper — signal readiness to engage the Kenya AI governance process.

AI economic impact and labor displacement in LMICs

Three signal pieces this month: CGD analysis argues AI's growth impact in advanced economies could be more than twice that in low-income countries due to infrastructure, skills, and institutional gaps. VoxDev notes developing economies face higher barriers to capturing AI benefits while having lower immediate displacement risk (because more manual and agricultural work has limited AI exposure). A new estimate flags that up to 60% of jobs in Bangladesh's garment sector could eventually be at risk — while ASEAN data from 2018–2022 showed industrial robot adoption created 2M skilled jobs but displaced 1.4M low-skilled workers. The International AI Safety Report 2026 (arXiv) flags capacity gaps in social safety nets in lower-income countries as a key vulnerability.

So what for 2AI: This evidence base is exactly what 2AI's forthcoming research agenda should engage — the inequality-amplification risk of AI in LMICs is both a theoretical framing challenge and a practical deployment design constraint. The Bangladesh garment sector figure, if it holds up, would be headline material in a 2AI position paper on responsible AI for development.
