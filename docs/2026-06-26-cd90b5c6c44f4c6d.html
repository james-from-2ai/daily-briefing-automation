On-device / edge LLMs: Gemma 4 for last-mile deployment

Google released Gemma 4 with E2B and E4B variants specifically built for phones and edge devices — multimodal (text, image, audio), 128K context, running on standard Android hardware. NVIDIA's deployment blog confirms Gemma 4 runs on Jetson and mobile NPUs. Google's LiteRT-LM runtime delivers 2.2× speedup via speculative decoding and uses a custom wNa8o8 schema engineered for low-RAM devices. The E2B/E4B models are the successors to Gemma 3n, removing the "must have good connectivity" constraint for LMIC deployments. MedGemma 4B (the medical-tuned variant) status in June 2026 is less clear from public sources, but Gemma 4 E4B with multimodal image input opens camera-based diagnostic workflows (X-ray screening, wound assessment) on mid-range Android. So what for 2AI: Gemma 4 E4B is the first model that plausibly meets 2AI's "offline, sub-4GB RAM, camera input, voice" deployment spec across all three workstreams simultaneously.
