CHW AI integration — what the Lancet Primary Care field survey just said

A May 2026 Lancet Primary Care study surveyed 28 NGOs running CHW programmes across 18 countries on AI readiness. Headline: CHW programmes' ability to adopt AI depends almost entirely on whether the surrounding system — clinics, supervision, supply chains, data systems, governance — is ready to integrate it, not on the AI itself. This frames AI-for-CHW not as a tooling problem but as a systems-integration problem. Adjacent funder moves reinforce the timing: the Gates / Novo Nordisk / Wellcome $60M EVAH initiative (Feb 2026) is funding locally-led evaluations of AI decision-support tools for frontline workers across sub-Saharan Africa, South Asia, and Southeast Asia, and PATH has plans for a phase-3 LLM-CDSS trial in Africa by year-end. So what for 2AI: the field is moving from "does it work" to "does the system support it" — any 2AI CHW pitch needs an explicit systems-readiness section, not just an efficacy claim.
