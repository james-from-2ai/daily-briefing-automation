AI economic impact & labor in LMICs

The IMF just published a major new Staff Discussion Note, "New Jobs Creation in the AI Age" (SDN/2026/001), specifically analyzing AI's employment effects in developing countries. Key finding: LMICs face 26–40% job exposure to AI (vs. 60% in advanced economies), but this lower exposure is structural — manual and agricultural jobs are less immediately at risk, not more protected. The more dangerous dynamic is a widening inequality gap: LMICs lack the infrastructure and skilled workforce to capture AI's productivity gains, meaning the technology could accelerate divergence. A study on ASEAN robot adoption (2018–22) found net effects: 2M skilled formal jobs created, 1.4M low-skilled formal jobs displaced. Women in East Asia and the Pacific face disproportionate AI displacement. VoxDev's emerging economy labor analysis argues the decisive variable is whether LMIC governments invest in AI absorptive capacity now.

So what for 2AI: The IMF SDN is a new anchor document for framing 2AI's intervention rationale to funders — specifically the argument that 2AI helps LMICs build AI absorptive capacity, not just deploy tools. Worth a 1-page brief translating SDN/2026/001 findings to 2AI's program implications.
