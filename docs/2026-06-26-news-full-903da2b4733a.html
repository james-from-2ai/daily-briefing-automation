National AI policy in LMICs: India, Kenya, African Union

India's IndiaAI Mission is actively onboarding state-level deployments — agriculture, health, and education are the three lead sectors for the 2026–27 priority pipeline. Kenya's AI Strategy (published late 2025) includes an explicit AI for agriculture mandate under the Ministry of Agriculture; the Kenya ICT Authority is now the designated AI regulatory body. The African Union's Continental AI Strategy was formally adopted in early 2026, with LMIC data sovereignty as a core principle — and a requirement that AI models used in African health systems be validated on local data before deployment. So what for 2AI: The AU Continental Strategy's validation requirement is a direct mandate for 2AI's CEA methodology — LMIC governments will increasingly require the kind of evidence framework 2AI builds.

AI economic impact: WDR 2026 labor chapter + ILO paper

The ILO–World Bank joint paper (released alongside WDR 2026) finds that AI-driven disruption in developing economies is likely to materialize faster than productivity gains because of the existing digital gaps — the opposite pattern from high-income countries where productivity gains lead disruption. The paper estimates 10–30% performance degradation when AI models trained on high-income country data are deployed in LMIC contexts without local validation. A NBER working paper on AI and service-sector jobs in India (published this spring) found measurable displacement in IT services, with limited new job creation to compensate. So what for 2AI: The performance degradation finding is the strongest quantitative argument for 2AI's local-validation work — embed it in every pitch deck that addresses the "just use ChatGPT" objection.
