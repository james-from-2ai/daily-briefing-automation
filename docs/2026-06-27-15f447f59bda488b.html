AI weather forecasting: NOAA deploys AIGFS + El Niño 2026 stress test

NOAA has operationally deployed a new suite of AI-driven global weather prediction models: AIGFS (AI Global Forecast System), AIGEFS (AI Global Ensemble Forecast System), and HGEFS (Hybrid-GEFS). The AIGFS runs a 16-day forecast using just 0.3% of the compute of the traditional GFS and completes in ~40 minutes, with improved tropical cyclone track accuracy at longer lead times. AIGEFS extends forecast skill by 18–24 hours beyond the traditional ensemble. Crucially, all three are now operational — not experimental — meaning NOAA's production forecasts now routinely incorporate AI. The timing matters: El Niño 2026 has an 82% probability of emergence by July and 96% persistence through winter, creating a live, high-stakes test of these models for anticipatory action across East Africa, South Asia, and Southeast Asia. NOAA press release.
