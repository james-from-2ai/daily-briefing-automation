AI + climate anticipatory action: track vs. intensity trade-offs

With hurricane season opening June 1, NOAA has confirmed AI weather models are being integrated as operational guidance — primarily for track forecasting (reliable) rather than intensity forecasting (systematically under-predicting extreme events). A Science Advances paper formalizes this asymmetry. For anticipatory action systems in tropical LMICs — where interventions are triggered by forecast thresholds — this means: AI-based triggers are appropriate for population movement/evacuation (track-dependent) but not for damage-severity planning (intensity-dependent). The ECMWF AI Weather Quest evaluation platform is benchmarking models against operational standards, providing a public evidence base for forecast-trigger calibration. So what for 2AI: Any 2AI anticipatory action work that uses AI weather triggers should specify which forecast type (track vs. intensity) is being used and justify accordingly. This is a citable methodological distinction that will matter for funders and evaluators.
