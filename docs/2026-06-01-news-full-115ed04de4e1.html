AI governance & national policy in Africa: 2026 state of play

Rwanda and Nigeria have published national AI strategies; Kenya and South Africa are still developing drafts. An OECD multistakeholder workshop in Nairobi (March 2026), co-hosted by Kenya’s Ministry of ICT, convened government representatives from 12 African countries to work through agriculture, language models, and regulation. The African Union’s Continental AI Strategy frames AI as a strategic priority at the continental level (see AU press release). Key governance gaps: limited institutional capacity, fragmented digital policy, low AI literacy, and persistent data-colonialism concerns. So what for 2AI: Kenya’s active policy development makes it the most important jurisdiction to track for 2AI’s intervention-design work. The OECD Nairobi workshop outputs are worth requesting — they directly feed the Kenya regulatory environment 2AI operates in.

AI lab LMIC government partnerships: who is signing what

Anthropic has a partnership with the Rwandan government on AI deployment. Microsoft has an initiative in Kenya. OpenAI launched an accelerator in India. Google is funding the Masakhane African Languages AI Hub to expand AI access across 40+ African languages. The Gates Foundation’s “Catalyzing Equitable AI Use” initiative supported 50 projects across Africa, Asia, and South America in health, education, agriculture, finance, and gender inclusion (see Stimson Center, 2026). Private-public partnerships are accelerating but remain clustered in flagship capitals (Nairobi, Kigali, Lagos, Accra). So what for 2AI: The AI lab partnership landscape is crowding out smaller organizations in flagship markets. 2AI should consider which partner relationships give it differentiated access and where the lab-free zones are for independent 2AI positioning.

AI in humanitarian response: capability vs. deployment gap

OpenAI, Google DeepMind, and Anthropic are all intensifying competition in agentic AI (see Axios, May 2026). However, application of these capabilities to humanitarian contexts (conflict response, displacement, food insecurity) remains thin — the lab focus is enterprise and consumer, not crisis response. The IGC’s “From promise to practice” piece documents the gap between AI capability and LMIC government deployment readiness. So what for 2AI: The humanitarian response sector is an underserved white space for serious implementation — labs aren’t building for it, and most INGO work is proof-of-concept. If 2AI has any humanitarian-response adjacencies in the portfolio, now is the time to build them out before the space crowds.

AI in education (Africa): World Bank EdTech + AI literacy gap

Google and UNICEF announced a three-year education partnership (from the briefing state: indexed May 30). The World Bank’s EdTech blog (World Bank, 2026) frames Africa’s demographic bulge as both the biggest challenge and the biggest opportunity for AI-enabled skilling. AI literacy training for teachers and students is emerging as a critical layer underneath tutoring-companion deployment. A separate thread: offline AI tutoring (Phi-3, Gemma small models) is now feasible for school tablet deployments without consistent internet. So what for 2AI: AI literacy training for educators is the unsexy but highest-leverage layer the flashier tutoring-companion work skips. 2AI’s education workstream should check whether any current programs include teacher AI literacy alongside the student-facing tools.

Weather × agriculture AI intersection: anticipatory action for farmers

The convergence of AI weather forecasting (NVIDIA Earth-2, ECMWF models) with AI ag advisory (CropFix, Virtual Agronomist) creates a new integrated use case: weather-triggered advisory bots that push pest/disease risk warnings to farmers 7-14 days ahead of expected conditions. This is the “anticipatory action for farmers” concept — analogous to anticipatory action in humanitarian response, applied to smallholder agriculture. No major deployment has been announced publicly yet, but the technical components (sub-seasonal forecasting + WhatsApp advisory) are both live in East Africa. So what for 2AI: Connecting the weather workstream with the ag workstream is a natural 2AI differentiator — it’s a space the labs aren’t building and the ag-only players don’t have the forecasting layer. This is a plausible basis for a 2AI concept note to funders.
