AI in global health (LMIC) — CHW deployments and the evidence gap

A mapping of 38 AI systems deployed with community health workers in LMICs finds that the dominant deployment category — over 40% of programs — is LLM-powered chatbots acting as always-on supervisors. Separately, Gates Foundation and OpenAI announced Horizon 1000, a $50M commitment to deploy AI tools across 1,000 primary care clinics in Africa by 2028, starting in Rwanda. The evidence base remains thin: only 4 of 86 RCTs on AI health tools globally (2018–2023) took place in LMICs. The ICTworks analysis on CHW distrust of AI is a useful corrective, naming supply-chain and referral system failures that AI chatbots cannot address.

So what for 2AI: The Horizon 1000 launch creates a credibility vacuum for independent evaluation — 2AI should stake out an evaluation-framework position before a funder fills it.

On-device / offline LLMs — the last-mile feasibility frontier

Google released Gemma 4-12B on June 6, running effectively on 16GB VRAM and nearly matching the 26B version. On June 14, Google released Diffusion Gemma — an open-weight diffusion language model producing 256 tokens simultaneously, its first non-autoregressive Gemma. Google's AI Edge Gallery app now lets users run Gemma on iPhone or Android with no internet, making offline health and agriculture advisory viable on consumer hardware. The major labs have converged on a sub-4B model tier: Llama 3.2 (1B/3B), Phi-4 mini (3.8B), Qwen2.5 (0.5B–1.5B), and SmolLM2 (135M–1.7B) all target resource-constrained deployment.

So what for 2AI: The Gemma 4-12B release restarts the feasibility conversation for on-device AI advisory in contexts with intermittent connectivity — a core constraint in 2AI's ag and health workstreams. Worth updating the internal capability tracker with these benchmarks.

AI economic disruption in LMICs — the labor exposure story

About 40% of the global economy faces AI disruption exposure (IMF); LMICs are less equipped to buffer it — fewer resources for retraining, higher exposure in BPO and clerical sectors. Kenya alone has ~2 million digital workers, most in roles highly susceptible to automation. Two-thirds of new global workforce entrants through 2030 will come from India and Sub-Saharan Africa (WEF Future of Jobs 2025). Gender amplifies the risk: 4.7% of women's jobs face severe disruption vs 2.4% for men, concentrated in administrative roles. JPMorgan Chase confirmed AI-driven workforce displacement in February 2026; Dimon warned in March that the transition "may be quicker" than past technological shifts. A June 2026 ICTworks analysis on the gender productivity paradox frames this as reshaping LMIC economic landscapes, not just developed-economy labor markets.

So what for 2AI: AI labor disruption in LMICs is a CGIAR/Gates-relevant framing for 2AI's economic workstream that currently has no published position. A rapid brief on the Kenya BPO exposure + anticipatory retraining policy gap could land well with the Africa AI Fund and GSMA.
