Human–large language model collaboration in clinical medicine: a systematic review and meta-analysis ✅ peer-reviewed  NPJ Digital Medicine · 2026
Authors: Guoyong Wang et al.
H+AI composite diagnostic/management scores improved +4.88 percentage points vs human-only (95% CI +0.65 to +9.12); time efficiency showed no difference; documentation quality improved but factual error rates remained at 26–36%.
Method / sample: Systematic review + meta-analysis, 10 peer-reviewed studies; PRISMA 2020; through June 28, 2025.
Effect size: +4.88 pp composite score (significant); prediction interval (-31.65 to 41.42) indicates high real-world uncertainty.
For 2AI: The wide prediction interval is the key finding — H+AI works on average but is highly context-dependent. This is the honest framing 2AI should use when pitching AI-assisted CHW support: "promising but uncertain, requires rigorous evaluation." Workstream: health evidence base.
