AI in education for LMICs

Evidence for AI-enabled learning tools is accumulating fast in 2026. A World Bank-supported after-school AI tutoring programme in Edo State, Nigeria produced significant learning gains after six weeks; in Kenya nearly 400,000 children use EIDU's structured pedagogy platform. A randomised trial in Benin City showed +0.26 SD in math, equivalent to 1.2–1.7 years of LMIC learning progress. India's Shiksha Copilot (1,000+ Karnataka government-school teachers) cut lesson-planning time and stress while improving documented lesson quality. Active World Bank pilots now span Côte d'Ivoire, The Gambia, Mali, Ghana, Ethiopia, Tanzania, and Mauritius. The EdTech Hub March 2026 synthesis flags that AI products built for high-income contexts often underperform for lower-income students.

So what for 2AI: Scrutinise whether pilots measure learning outcomes or just adoption, especially in low-connectivity contexts.
