Qwen3.7-Max and Qwen3.7-Plus — 1 million-token context at commodity pricing

Alibaba released Qwen3.7-Max on May 20, 2026 at the Alibaba Cloud Summit, positioning it as an agent-first model designed to run for up to 35 hours and execute over 1,000 consecutive tool calls without performance degradation. The context window is 1 million tokens (text only, no image input) — double the prior generation. API pricing: $2.50 input / $7.50 output per million tokens, with $0.25 cached-input pricing that makes repeated-context workflows materially cheaper. On June 1 Alibaba added Qwen3.7-Plus, which layers in vision (image understanding) and deep reasoning alongside the full tool-invocation suite. Both models are cross-listed on OpenRouter, Together AI, and Alibaba Cloud. The practical implication: a 1M-context, vision-capable model is now available at a price point that makes multi-document reasoning — e.g., ingesting an entire program corpus and generating a cost-effectiveness brief — feasible without a premium frontier model.

So what for 2AI: Qwen3.7-Max is worth testing for the corpus-scale CE modeling task (MiniMax M3 was proposed last week as an alternative). At $2.50/M input it undercuts GPT-4-level pricing significantly. Evaluate for the LMEF document-analysis pipeline and the agriculture-Substack research synthesis flows.

South Africa AI policy: withdrawn after hallucinated-citations scandal

South Africa's Draft National AI Policy — gazetted April 10, 2026 with a June 10 public comment deadline — was withdrawn by Communications Minister Solly Malatsi after investigators found the document contained fabricated academic references, in what appears to be a case where the drafting process itself relied on AI-generated text that hallucinated its own citations. The 60-day public comment period is now moot; Fasken's commentary captures the structural irony: a national AI policy document discredited by the exact failure mode it was designed to address. A revised draft is expected but no new timeline has been announced. South Africa's withdrawal leaves a gap in the AU's push to have member states adopt national AI frameworks before the 2026 AU Summit.

So what for 2AI: This is a concrete, citable case study for 2AI's governance advisory work — AI tools used in policy drafting without adequate human verification, producing documents that erode rather than build institutional trust. When the revised South Africa policy opens for comment (likely Q3 2026), 2AI should be positioned to contribute the development-sector perspective on AI deployment requirements in LMIC health and agriculture settings.

Microsoft and Google escalate the coding-agent race

At Build 2026 (June 1–2), Microsoft released MAI-Code-1-Flash, its first model that generates application code from written descriptions, explicitly designed to reduce developer reliance on OpenAI at lower per-token cost. Google countered with Gemini 3.5 Flash (released alongside Antigravity 2.0), benchmarked as the lowest-cost frontier model for coding and agent tasks. Both compete directly with Anthropic's Claude Code, which has been the primary driver of Anthropic's revenue acceleration. For practitioners building agentic pipelines (not just consumer chat), the market now has three well-resourced frontier options — each pushing toward cheaper, faster tool-use chains with longer context.

So what for 2AI: 2AI's briefing automation and CE modeling pipeline currently runs on Claude.
