AI in education (LMIC) — tutoring + teacher copilots

The Anthropic-Gates $200M partnership explicitly includes K-12 AI tutoring in sub-Saharan Africa, India, and the US, anchoring AI-in-education as a major investment priority for 2026–2029. Separately, a wave of LMIC-specific tutoring deployments is maturing: the Rwanda AI tutoring model (Bridge/NewGlobe-adjacent) is now cited in Gates program descriptions as a reference case; World Bank EdTech is publishing guidance on AI tutoring for low-bandwidth settings. The key open question across all deployments is whether learning gains persist at scale beyond the pilot phase. So what for 2AI: The education workstream is entering the same evaluation-pressure phase that health AI is already in — funders will start requiring rigor. 2AI should begin framing its education work in evaluation terms now rather than waiting for the first funder RFP.

On-device / offline LLMs — small model frontier

Google's MedGemma (medical small model, deployable on-device) was released earlier in 2026 as part of the Gemma family. Phi-4 (Microsoft) and Llama 3.3 variants continue to push performance-per-GB lower, with sub-4GB models now achieving GPT-3.5-level task performance. TranslateGemma provides multilingual support in 100+ languages at sub-1B parameter size, with decent performance on Swahili, Hindi, Hausa, and Amharic — core 2AI languages. The "offline AI" deployment problem for the last mile is functionally solved for many tasks; the remaining challenge is data freshness and workflow integration. So what for 2AI: MedGemma is worth testing against 2AI's health workstream tasks — it's specifically trained on medical reasoning and can run on a mid-range Android device. This strengthens the LMIC deployment case in settings without reliable internet.

AI governance for LMICs — the emerging gap

The UN High-Level Advisory Body on AI published its final report in mid-2024 but implementation remains fragmented. As of June 2026, no major LMIC government has a published AI governance framework specifically addressing AI tool deployment in health and agriculture (as distinct from general AI policy). India is closest with IndiaAI Mission's sector guidance, but it's general. The EU AI Act's extraterritorial effects are starting to be discussed for LMIC AI deployment, but no enforcement case has emerged. So what for 2AI: "AI governance for LMIC deployment contexts" is genuinely unclaimed territory — no NGO, no lab, no multilateral has published a practical framework. A 5-page "practitioner guide to responsible AI deployment for development organizations" could establish 2AI as the reference point. This is a 1–2 week writing project with high citation potential.

AI for maternal + child health — specific LMIC deployments

Penda Health (Kenya) and similar clinical AI systems continue to be cited in Gates/Wellcome literature as reference cases for AI-enabled frontline triage. WHO's SMART Guidelines initiative is integrating AI recommendations into digital health standards for maternal care in LMICs. The EVAH initiative specifically calls out maternal health, TB, and malaria as priority use cases. Notable gap: no large-scale RCT on AI maternal health tools has published results as of June 2026 — most evidence is pre-post or observational. So what for 2AI: Maternal health AI + RCT design is exactly the EVAH Pathway B sweet spot. If 2AI has a health system partner in Kenya or India with maternal care data access, this is the specific application to build a Pathway B proposal around.

AI for anticipatory action + disaster response

The 2026 El Niño season is fueling increased attention on AI-powered anticipatory action. OCHA and Red Cross are piloting AI early-warning systems that trigger pre-positioned humanitarian response before disasters hit. The AIM for Scale weather advisory work (4 countries, 2026) overlaps with this but focuses on agricultural rather than humanitarian response. ECMWF's AI weather models (AIFS, successor to IFS) are now operational in limited contexts, providing sub-kilometer resolution forecasting in parts of Africa. So what for 2AI: The anticipatory action and AI weather advisory converge on the same country programs — if 2AI's weather workstream is active in Kenya or Ethiopia, the anticipatory action funders (OCHA, Red Cross, Start Fund) are a natural expansion path beyond agricultural advisory.

AI economic impact in LMICs — labor displacement signal

A cluster of working papers (World Bank, ILO, GSMA) published in H1 2026 on AI and labor in LMICs: the emerging consensus is that task-level automation risk in LMICs is lower than in HICs (because many at-risk HIC tasks — data processing, customer service — are either already automated or done informally in LMICs), but that AI-enabled workflow changes will hit formal employment sectors (BPO, banking, public administration) hard and fast. The more pressing LMIC risk is the concentration of AI economic gains in countries/organizations with data advantage. So what for 2AI: This is relevant context for 2AI's funder narrative — the LMIC AI opportunity isn't just about using AI to deliver health/agriculture services; it's about ensuring LMICs capture AI's productivity gains rather than just absorbing its displacement effects. Frame this in the cG meeting as 2AI's macro theory of change.
